After years of thinking "I should write about this", I finally set up a blog section on my site.

The goal is simple: write about things I actually work with — distributed systems, backend architecture, developer tooling, and the occasional deep dive into something that surprised me. No tutorials for the sake of tutorials. Just notes from the trenches.

## Why now?

I've been noticing that writing forces a level of clarity that thinking alone doesn't. When you try to explain something precisely, the gaps show up fast. That's useful.

Also, I've been integrating AI-assisted coding tools heavily into my workflow lately, and I want a place to document what actually works and what doesn't — beyond the marketing copy.

## What to expect

Short, technical posts. Probably irregular cadence. No newsletter, no tracking, no dark patterns.

If something is useful to you, great. If not, that's fine too.
